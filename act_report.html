Hello reader, Thank you for reading my article. In this article, I'll be talking about my second project for Udacity Data Analyst nano-degree.

For this project we were required to put our data wrangling abilities to the test. We were required to gather data from different sources, wrangle the data and analyse the data to produce insights. For those of us who do not know what data wrangling is, I will go ahead to give a brief definition of data wrangling.

Data wrangling is the process of cleaning, organizing and transforming raw data into desired formats for analysis. The goal of data wrangling is to assure quality and useful data.

The data for this project was provided by Udacity. The datasets came in 3 different formats, a CSV, TSV and JSON. Two of the datasets contained information from a page 'WeRateDogs' on twitter. Information such as tweet Id, favorite count, etc. The other dataset contained dog breed predictions gotten by passing dog images through a deep learning AI.

After, I had gathered the datasets and assessed them, I was curious to know which dog breed was the most rated, which dog breeds got the most likes, whether the number of images uploaded influenced the number of likes and the year the page got the most engagements and the dog stage that got the most engagements. So I kept this questions in mind as I went through the data wrangling procedures.

After data cleaning and analysis, I was able to answer the questions I had in mind. The dog breeds that had the highest ratings was found to be Bouvier des Flandres and valley followed closely by Saluki, briard and Tibetan mastiff. The visualization below will further clarify this.

breeds_df = df[df['breeds'] != 'NaN']

plt.title('Top Rated Dog Breeds')
plt.xlabel('Rating')
plt.ylabel('Breeds');

In the above visual we can see that Valley and Bouvier des Flandres are the most rated dog breeds followed by Saluki, Tibetan mastiff, briard and Border terrier. Let's use a word cloud to checkout why this page gave this dog breeds this high ratings.

df['date'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date'])
df['year'] = pd.DatetimeIndex(df['date']).year
date_plot = df.groupby('year')[['favorite_count', 'retweet_count']].mean()
fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(10, 5))
date_plot.plot(kind = 'bar', ax=ax)
plt.title('Mean Retweet and Favorite count per year')
plt.ylabel('Mean count');

It is clearly depicted above that the WeRateDogs page had the most engagements in year 2017 with a mean favorite count of about 25,000 and retweet count of about 5000. This may be attributed to increased popularity of the page as the followers count remained almost the same within the time period.

To the question, Was the favorite count influenced by the number of images uploaded? The answer is 'No' as can be seen below. Although there both are positively correlated, the number of images uploaded did not have any significant influence on the favorite count.
